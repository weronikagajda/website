weronika gajda | projects | activities

I’m an interdisciplinary PhD researcher at the Faculties of Architecture and Engineering Technology at KU Leuven in Ghent. Before my master’s studies, I worked as an architect at medusagroup in Poland. I’m affiliated with the research groups Architecture & Wicked Matters, Geomatics, and DRAMCO.

My research project, Citography, investigates how digital media ecologies influence urban living and design. It explores how image sharing, social media, mobile devices, geolocation, surveying, computer vision and data visualization affect how we understand, map and design cities — and how they shape notions of place, commons and citizenship.

In my mapping practice, I develop the Citographer Toolkit, including tempo-spatial modeling with Blender, sensing tools design, and data acquisition techniques. The toolkit supports exploring how technologies shape public space experience and design. Throughout my PhD, I teach a mapping elective titled “Citography” to architecture students.
